Can Lumbar Disc Herniation Be Treated Without Surgery Using Chuna Therapy?

📅 April 6, 2026 ✍️ Dr. Lee Sang-jin 🏥 Bit Korean Medicine Clinic

Do You Really Need Surgery for a Lumbar Disc?

Sudden back pain, a feeling of pain and numbness shooting down to your legs… Are you wondering if you have a lumbar disc herniation? Many people hear the word "disc" and immediately panic about surgery. In reality, however, most patients with lumbar disc herniation can significantly improve their symptoms without surgery. One increasingly recognized alternative is Chuna therapy.

What Is Chuna Therapy?

Chuna therapy is a Korean medicine manual therapy in which the practitioner uses their hands, body weight, or assistive tools to correct misaligned spinal and joint structures. It is not simply about "adjusting bones" — the core of the treatment is releasing tension in the muscles and ligaments around the spine, reducing pressure on the disc, and supporting natural recovery.

Why Chuna Therapy Helps Lumbar Disc Herniation

  • Corrects spinal alignment to reduce disc compression.
  • Relieves tension in surrounding muscles and ligaments, reducing pain.
  • Can help improve leg numbness caused by nerve compression.
  • Enhances the body's self-healing capacity, helping prevent recurrence.

Who Is a Good Candidate for Chuna Therapy?

Chuna therapy is especially recommended for:

  • Those whose MRI shows disc protrusion but have not been told surgery is necessary
  • Those who have radiating pain extending from the lower back to the hip and legs
  • Those whose back feels heavy and stiff after prolonged sitting or standing
  • Those who still have pain after surgery and need additional treatment

However, if the disc is severely ruptured or there is significant neurological impairment such as lower limb paralysis, a specialist's detailed diagnosis takes priority. Because the scope of Chuna therapy varies based on individual conditions, it is important to proceed only after a thorough consultation.
