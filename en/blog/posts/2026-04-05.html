Cervical Disc Herniation Is Not Just a Stiff Neck

Have you ever woken up with a stiff neck and a tingling sensation running down to your fingertips? It's easy to dismiss it as fatigue or muscle tension, but if these symptoms keep coming back, it may be worth considering cervical disc herniation (cervical intervertebral disc protrusion).

Cervical disc herniation occurs when the disc between the cervical vertebrae slips out of position and compresses nearby nerves. With smartphones and computers now a part of daily life, this condition affects people of all ages.

Common Symptoms of Cervical Disc Herniation

  • Chronic stiffness and heaviness in the neck and shoulders
  • Numbness or reduced sensation in one or both arms and hands
  • Frequent tension headaches at the back of the head
  • Pain that worsens when turning or tilting the head
  • A feeling of weakness in the arms in severe cases

If two or more of these apply to you, we recommend seeking an accurate diagnosis from a specialist.

How Does Korean Medicine Treat It?

Acupuncture & Pharmacopuncture

Effective for relaxing tense muscles and ligaments and reducing inflammation. Pharmacopuncture in particular involves injecting herbal medicine compounds directly into acupoints to help nerve recovery.

Chuna Therapy

Corrects misaligned cervical vertebrae to reduce pressure on the disc. Rather than simply relieving pain, this is a treatment that addresses structural root causes.

Herbal Medicine

Prescriptions tailored to the individual's constitution and symptoms help regulate inflammation and support the recovery of damaged tissue.
