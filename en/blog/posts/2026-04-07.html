Frozen Shoulder Acupuncture: Effects & Recovery Timeline

📅 April 7, 2026 ✍️ Dr. Lee Sang-jin 🏥 Bit Korean Medicine Clinic

Does Frozen Shoulder Heal on Its Own?

Frozen shoulder, formally known as adhesive capsulitis, is a condition in which inflammation develops in the joint capsule surrounding the shoulder joint and the tissue adheres, causing simultaneous pain and restricted range of motion. People often say "it heals on its own over time," but left untreated, recovery can take 2–3 years, and in the meantime daily life can become seriously impaired. Starting appropriate treatment early is key to shortening the recovery period.

What Does Acupuncture Do for Frozen Shoulder?

In Korean medicine, frozen shoulder is understood as a state in which circulation of qi and blood around the shoulder is obstructed and the muscles and ligaments have stiffened. Acupuncture promotes blood circulation in the affected local area, alleviates the inflammatory response, and releases tension in stiffened tissue.

  • Pain relief: Stimulating acupoints around the shoulder regulates nerve excitability that causes pain.
  • Restoration of range of motion: Releases tension in the stiffened joint capsule and surrounding muscles, gradually restoring the ability to raise the arm or rotate it backward.
  • Reduction of inflammation: Acupuncture stimulation induces an anti-inflammatory response, helping to reduce inflammation within the joint capsule.

Acupuncture is sometimes performed alone, but combining it with bee venom pharmacopuncture, Chuna therapy, or thermotherapy often produces even better results.

How Long Does Recovery Take?

Recovery time for frozen shoulder varies significantly depending on the severity of symptoms and when treatment begins. In general, if treatment starts early, significant improvement can be expected within 3–6 months. In chronic cases, consistent management over 6 months to a year or more may be needed.

Stage-by-Stage Recovery

  • Acute phase (1–3 months): Pain is severe and shoulder movement is very difficult. During this period, focus on pain relief and avoid overexertion.
  • Recovery phase (3–6 months): Pain decreases and shoulder range of motion gradually increases. Combining acupuncture with gentle stretching is effective.
  • Stabilization phase (after 6 months): Function recovers to a level that allows normal daily activities, but consistent management remains important to prevent recurrence.
